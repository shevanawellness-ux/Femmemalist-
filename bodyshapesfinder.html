DETAILED BODY SHAPE FINDER

WHY BODY SHAPE MATTERS

Body shape describes how your shoulders, bust, waist and hips are proportioned relative to each other.

Understanding your body shape helps you choose clothing styles that create balance and highlight your natural proportions.

Stylists usually categorize body shapes into five main types:

• Hourglass
• Pear (Triangle)
• Apple (Round)
• Rectangle (Straight)
• Inverted Triangle

STEP 1 — PREPARE FOR MEASUREMENT

Follow these instructions for accurate measurements:

• Wear fitted clothing or underwear
• Stand straight with relaxed posture
• Keep feet together
• Use a flexible measuring tape
• Do not pull the tape too tightly

Tip: Measurements should reflect your natural body shape. Avoid holding your breath or tightening your stomach.

STEP 2 — MEASURE YOUR BODY

Shoulder Width

Measure across the widest part of your shoulders.

• Start at the outer edge of one shoulder
• Extend the tape across your back
• End at the outer edge of the other shoulder

Bust

Measure around the fullest part of your chest.

• Wrap the tape around the fullest point
• Keep tape level across your back

Waist

Measure the narrowest part of your waist.

• Usually above the belly button
• Below the rib cage

Hips

Measure around the widest part of your hips and buttocks.

• Keep feet together
• Wrap tape around the fullest part

STEP 3 — OBSERVE YOUR BODY PROPORTIONS

1. Compare shoulders and hips.

If shoulders and hips are similar → Possible hourglass or rectangle.
If hips are wider → Likely pear.
If shoulders are wider → Likely inverted triangle.

2. Look at your waist.

If waist curves strongly inward → Hourglass or pear.
If waist is slightly inward → Rectangle.
If waist appears fuller or outward → Apple.

3. Where does your body appear widest?

Upper body → Inverted triangle.
Midsection → Apple.
Hips → Pear.
Balanced → Hourglass or rectangle.

4. Where do you gain weight first?

Stomach area → Apple.
Hips and thighs → Pear.
Evenly distributed → Rectangle.
Balanced curves → Hourglass.

5. Look at your silhouette in the mirror.

Strong curves at waist → Hourglass.
Straight outline → Rectangle.
Top wider than bottom → Inverted triangle.
Bottom wider than top → Pear.
Rounded midsection → Apple.

BODY SHAPE RESULTS

Hourglass
Shoulders and hips are similar width. Waist is significantly narrower. Body has balanced curves.

Pear (Triangle)
Hips are wider than shoulders. Waist is defined. Lower body appears fuller.

Apple
Midsection appears fuller. Waist measurement is similar to bust or hips.

Rectangle
Shoulders, waist and hips are similar. Waist definition is minimal.

Inverted Triangle
Shoulders or bust are wider than hips. Upper body appears broader.

EXTRA SILHOUETTE TEST

Stand sideways in front of a mirror.

Observe your outline:

• Does your waist curve sharply inward?
• Are your hips noticeably wider?
• Are your shoulders the widest part?
• Is your body mostly straight? These visual clues help confirm your body shape.
