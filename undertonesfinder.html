FIND YOUR SKIN UNDERTONE

WHAT IS A SKIN UNDERTONE?

Your undertone is the subtle color beneath the surface of your skin. Unlike your skin tone, which may change due to tanning or sun exposure, your undertone usually stays the same throughout life.

Understanding your undertone helps you choose clothing colors, makeup shades, jewelry, and accessories that naturally enhance your appearance.

There are four primary undertones:

Cool Undertone – pink, red, or bluish tones

Warm Undertone – golden, peach, or yellow tones

Neutral Undertone – balanced warm and cool tones

Olive Undertone – subtle green or muted undertones

PRACTICAL UNDERTONE TEST

Answer the following questions by observing your skin in natural light.

1. Look at the veins on your wrist.
A. Blue or purple
B. Green
C. Both blue and green
D. Slightly green-grey
2. Try wearing silver and gold jewelry.
A. Silver looks better
B. Gold looks better
C. Both look good
D. Both look good but muted metals look best
3. Hold pure white and cream clothing near your face.
A. Pure white looks better
B. Cream looks better
C. Both look fine
D. Both look slightly off
4. How does your skin react to the sun?
A. Burns easily
B. Tans easily
C. Sometimes burns and sometimes tans
D. Tans but sometimes looks slightly ashy
5. Try berry lipstick and coral lipstick.
A. Berry looks natural
B. Coral looks natural
C. Both look fine
D. Many lipsticks look too bright
6. When matching foundation:
A. Yellow foundations look too warm
B. Pink foundations look too cool
C. Neutral foundations work best
D. Most foundations look wrong
7. Hold silver foil and gold foil near your face.
A. Silver brightens skin
B. Gold brightens skin
C. Both look similar
D. Both look okay but muted shades look better
8. Your natural hair tone appears:
A. Ashy brown or cool black
B. Golden brown or warm brown
C. Neutral brown
D. Slightly muted or olive toned
9. Which clothing colors look best?
A. Blue, purple, jewel tones
B. Orange, coral, warm tones
C. Most colors work
D. Earthy tones like olive and moss
10. In natural daylight your skin looks:
A. Rosy or pinkish
B. Golden or yellow
C. Balanced
D. Slightly greenish or muted

UNDERSTANDING YOUR RESULTS

Mostly A – Cool Undertone
Your skin likely contains pink or bluish undertones. Colors like sapphire, lavender, cool pink, and silver jewelry usually complement cool undertones.

Mostly B – Warm Undertone
Your skin likely contains golden or peach undertones. Warm colors like coral, bronze, terracotta, and gold jewelry often enhance warm undertones.

Mostly C – Neutral Undertone
Your skin likely has a balance of warm and cool tones. Both warm and cool colors may suit you well.

Mostly D – Olive Undertone
Your skin likely contains subtle green or muted tones. Olive undertones often look best with earthy colors such as olive green, moss, terracotta, and deep neutrals.

WHY UNDERTONES MATTER

When colors match your undertone, your skin appears brighter and more balanced.

Understanding your undertone can help you choose:

• flattering clothing colors
• better foundation shades
• jewelry metals that suit your skin
• makeup palettes that enhance your natural features
