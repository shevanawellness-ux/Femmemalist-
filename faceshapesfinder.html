FIND YOUR FACE SHAPE

WHAT IS YOUR FACE SHAPE?

Your face shape influences which hairstyles, glasses, makeup techniques and accessories look most flattering on you.

Instead of guessing, this guide helps you determine your face shape using simple measurements that stylists commonly use.

STEP 1 — PREPARE FOR MEASUREMENT

Before measuring your face, follow these steps:

• Pull your hair completely away from your face
• Stand in front of a mirror in natural light
• Use a flexible measuring tape (or string and ruler)
• Measure in centimeters or inches

You will take four measurements: face length, forehead width, cheekbone width, and jawline width.

STEP 2 — MEASURE YOUR FACE

1. Face Length

Measure from the middle of your hairline to the tip of your chin.

• Start at the center of your hairline
• Measure straight down the center of your face
• Stop at the lowest point of your chin

2. Forehead Width

Measure the widest part of your forehead.

• Place the measuring tape slightly above your eyebrows
• Measure from one temple to the other temple

3. Cheekbone Width

Measure across the highest points of your cheekbones.

• Start just below the outer corner of one eye
• Measure across the face to the same point on the other side

4. Jawline Width

This measurement is taken in two steps.

Step 1: Measure from the corner of your jaw (below your ear) to the center of your chin.

Step 2: Multiply that number by two.

This gives your total jawline width.

STEP 3 — OBSERVE YOUR FEATURES

1. Which measurement is the largest?
A. Face length
B. Cheekbones
C. Forehead
D. Jawline
2. Compare face length to face width.
A. Face length is clearly longer
B. Length and width are similar
C. Face width is larger
3. Look at your jawline.
A. Soft and rounded
B. Slightly curved
C. Sharp and angular
D. Narrow or pointed
4. Where is the widest part of your face?
A. Forehead
B. Cheekbones
C. Jawline
D. All similar
5. Look closely at your chin.
A. Rounded
B. Slightly narrow
C. Broad or flat
D. Pointed

FACE SHAPE RESULTS

Oval Face Shape
Face length is the largest measurement. Forehead is slightly wider than the jawline. Chin is softly rounded.

Round Face Shape
Face length and width are similar. Jawline is soft and rounded. Cheeks appear full.

Square Face Shape
Forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are similar in width. Jawline appears strong and angular.

Heart Face Shape
Forehead is the widest part of the face. Jawline becomes narrow toward a pointed chin.

Diamond Face Shape
Cheekbones are the widest part of the face. Forehead and jawline are narrower.

Rectangle (Oblong) Face Shape
Face length is significantly longer than width. Forehead, cheekbones, and jawline have similar widths.

EXTRA CHECK — MIRROR TRACING TEST

If you are unsure, try this simple method:

• Pull your hair away from your face
• Look into a mirror
• Use lipstick or a washable marker
• Trace the outline of your face on the mirror

The outline you draw will resemble your face shape.
